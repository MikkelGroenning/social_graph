Follow Graph Analysis

In this section result from the follow graph will be presented. The follow graph is constructed based on which profiles that follow eachother on Twitter. In other words if e.g. a Senator follows anoter Senator on Twitter a directed edge between them is constructed. The idea is illustrated in the image below. In this graph we consider the three politicans Dean Heller, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and Bernie Sanders as nodes. There is directed edge between Dean Heller and Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez that means that Dean Heller follows Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez on Twitter. As Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez does not follow Dean Heller there is not edge going the opposite direction. Following the annology it can be seen that Bernie Sanders follow Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and vice wheras Dean Heller follow Bernie Sander but Bernie Sanders does not follow Dean Heller. This is the type of graph that will be created for all 618 politicans in our dataset.

Constructing the Graph

The graph is constructed by looking up every politican in the dataset Twitter profile with Twitter API. Here it is exstracted who the profile follows and directed links are created between the politicans as described above. The total progress can be seen in Get_follow_Adjancency notebook or quickly access here.

The created graph contains 618 nodes but as three politicians who act as isolates they are removed - so the graph that will be considered contain 615 nodes. This graph contrain a total of 83778 edges. With that many edgedÂ´s the network can definitly be consideres dense as each politician follows approximately 137 of the other politicians, on average. This corresponds to around 22% of all polticians. Following this info it will be investigated if patterns can be seen in who follows who.

Community Detection

The last analysis that will be carried out on the graph is community detection and analysis. First communities based on the data about the politicians will be investigated before looking at the Louvain method to create communities based on the actual links in the who-follow-who graph.

The first natural split that will be investigated is based on parties. Recall that all but 3 node were either Democrat or Republican - namely the two independent politicians Bernie Sanders and Angus King as well as the liberiatarian politician Justin Amash. Here it will be explored which of the two large parties they are likely to support. The approach to this analysis will be to see how these three politicians should be placed to obtain the highest modularity. Now, for the three last politicians there are (\2^3=8\) combinations. Communities based on states

The next split will be based on state. The modularity obtained is 0.063 which is much wors that the party-split. There are though also now 56 communities where some are very small why it can make sense to try to increase it by merging the states.

Trying to merge states

Now the states are merge with the Louvain algorithm. This is done by first making a new a new weighted graph with 56 nodes - one corresponding to each state. The edges are now weighted where the weight between two states are given as the total number of links between politicians from these two states. This forces the first iteration in the Louvain method to first merge on a state level. Now treating the states as new nodes the algorithm can be used to find the best state parition. The result in communties accross States are shown on the map interactive map below.

It is interesting that they are their own community here. Lastly community 7 is a bit harder to explain.

The modularity of the state communties are 0.134 higher than only considering one state but it is still much worse that the party split.

Subconclusion

In this part, a graph-based on what politicians follow each other has been created, visualized, and analyzed. The graph was very dense! Overall it was found that representatives very clearly followed others from their own party while the senators were more likely to follow other senators. A lot of different tools for analyzing networks were used and the conclusion can be summarized to the fact that politicians are by a clear margin more likely to follow others with the same beliefs as themselves - this includes a split between senators and representatives too. When using the Louvain method three communities were found that can be summarized as senators, Republican representatives, and Democratic representatives.
