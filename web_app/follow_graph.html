Follow Graph Analysis

In this section result from the follow graph will be presented. The follow graph is constructed based on which profiles that follow eachother on Twitter. In other words if e.g. a Senator follows anoter Senator on Twitter a directed edge between them is constructed. The idea is illustrated in the image below. In this graph we consider the three politicans Dean Heller, Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and Bernie Sanders as nodes. There is directed edge between Dean Heller and Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez that means that Dean Heller follows Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez on Twitter. As Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez does not follow Dean Heller there is not edge going the opposite direction. Following the annology it can be seen that Bernie Sanders follow Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and vice wheras Dean Heller follow Bernie Sander but Bernie Sanders does not follow Dean Heller. This is the type of graph that will be created for all 618 politicans in our dataset.

Constructing the Graph

The graph is constructed by looking up every politican in the dataset Twitter profile with Twitter API. Here it is exstracted who the profile follows and directed links are created between the politicans as described above. The total progress can be seen in Get_follow_Adjancency notebook or quickly access here.

The created graph contains 618 nodes but as three politicians who act as isolates they are removed - so the graph that will be considered contain 615 nodes. This graph contrain a total of 83778 edges. With that many edgedÂ´s the network can definitly be consideres dense as each politician follows approximately 137 of the other politicians, on average. This corresponds to around 22% of all polticians. Following this info it will be investigated if patterns can be seen in who follows who.

Assortativity

In the initial analysis of the edges, it was found how more likely politicians are to follow others from their own party compared to other parties. In this section the assortativity accross Parties, Type and State. Below can a table be seen that sumarizes the result.

Assortativity
Party 0.812
Type 0.576
State 0.065

The associativity of Nodes from same party is 0.81 - a very high measure. But not surprising given the structure of the graph. It clearly tells that politicians from the same parties likes to link up.

From the plot of the graph it also looks like the senators are sepeareted from the representatives. And it is confirmed with associativity of 0.58. Though it is lower than associativty accross parties it is still a clear positive assortativity meaning that the politicians like to link up to other politicians of the same type.

Across polticians from the same State a much lower value, though postivte assortativity is found 0.065. Off course there are also way more states, where many of them only have a few politician which off course makes it harder to find very high assortativity.
